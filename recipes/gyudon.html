Gyudon


Description

Think of gyudon as the ultimate Japanese comfort food — tender, thinly sliced beef simmered in a sweet-savory sauce, served over a steaming bowl of rice. Topped with pickled ginger, this dish is a warm hug in a bowl that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 small onion
  • 1/2 cup of hondashi
  • 1/4 cup dry sake
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 pound thinly shaved beef ribeye or chuck steak
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • salt
  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • sliced scallions
  • beni-shoga (pickedle ginger)

Directions

  1. Thinly slice the onions into thin slivers.
  2. Combine onion, hondashi, sugar, and soy sause in a medium pan and bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Continue cooking until the onion is soft.
  4. Add beef and continue cooking until the beef is fully cooked and the liquid has reduced to a concentrated broth.
  5. Serve in a bowl on warm cooked rice and add a little bit of beni-shoga and scallions as garnish.
